Lifting for BJJ

You might ask yourself why you should strength training for BJJ?

Even though the japanese phrase 'Jiu Jitsu' translates to 'Gentle Art', we all know that 'gentle' can be a bit of an understatement.
Whether you are competing or just train BJJ recreationally, you will inevitably have the wonderful experience of getting slammed to the mat, armbarred, shoulder-locked, kneebarred, ezekiel-choked, and much more fun stuff.

Strength training prepares our bodies for whatever we will face during practice or competition. And frankly, it is very necessary. Lifting will reduce alot of the risk that comes with BJJ by strengthening your muscles, joints and bones. In short, the less injury you get, the more fun you can have training.

An important element of BJJ is facing an opponent in sparring or competition. Having strength gives you the upper hand against your opponent(skills aside), which makes it a no-brainer to decide whether or not to strength train.

Benefits for BJJ Practicioners

The benefits of strength training for Jiu Jitsu are immense.

  • • Significantly less injuries
  • • Confidence in your body's capabilities
  • • More leverage in your game
  • • Reduction in time spent recovering
  • • Long term ability to train

Who Strength Trains?

BJJ athletes from all over the world recognize the importance of strength training

Jiu Jitsu greats like Gordon Ryan, André Galvao, Jacaré Souza and Gilbert Durinho Burns all recognize the major benefits of strength training for the sport of Brazilian Jiu Jitsu.

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu was originally formed as a combat method for the 'smaller' guy to be able to better their larger oponents. Larger oponents who were not aware of BJJ techniques. Nowadays, BJJ has become a global and well-known sport, which means that the advantages that the 'smaller' guy used to hold have deminished.

To be able to increase longevity in the sport, strength training is highly recommended.
